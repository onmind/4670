In 2008, Stephan Günzel observed the beginning of a paradigmatic shift in game studies, a paradigm in which games are consistently conceptualised as spatial objects.1

Paradigmatic shift: A significant change in how something is thought about or organized.

This shift has been instrumental in the technological advancement of game design, influencing not only how games are created but also how they are perceived by players.

Technological advancement: Development and application of tools, machines, materials, techniques, and sources of power to make work easier.

1. See also Aarseth, 2001a; Aarseth and Günzel, 2019; Murray, 1997.
